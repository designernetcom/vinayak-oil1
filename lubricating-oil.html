What is Lubricating Oils?

Lubricating Oils is a thick fatty oil used to make the parts of a machine move smoothly. Lubricating Oils creates a separating film between surfaces of adjacent moving parts to minimize direct contact between them, decreasing heat caused by friction and reducing wear. Lubricating Oils is used in motorized vehicles, where it is known specifically as motor oil and transmission fluid.

Lubricating Oils are essential for reducing friction, wear, and overheating in various machinery and engines. They are formulated from base oils and additives, providing properties that ensure smooth operation, extend equipment life, and improve efficiency.

Types of Lubricating Oils

Mineral oils

Mineral oils are Lubricating Oils refined from naturally occurring crude oil.

Synthetic oils

Synthetic oils are Lubricating Oils that are manufactured.
